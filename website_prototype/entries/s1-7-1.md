overview: |
  意义感调适分布最广，心理适应能力使"危机"不等于"普遍深度痛苦"，但适应过程因个体和社区条件显著不均。孤独流行病呈悖论形态：社区连接更密集而深度孤独更普遍——原因在连接的质量而非数量。慢性低度抑郁（Dysthymia）远比急性抑郁症普遍，不达临床干预门槛但持续降低生活质量，在多态社会因缺少生存压力的覆盖而充分浮现。
title_en: Principal Mental Health Vectors
summary_en: Chronic Low-Grade Depression becomes the most common clinical presentation — "nothing particularly wrong, but nothing particularly good either."
overview_en: |
  Adjustment of the sense of meaning is the most widely distributed; psychological adaptability means "crisis" does not equal widespread deep suffering, but the adaptation process is markedly uneven across individual and community conditions. The loneliness epidemic takes a paradoxical form: community connection grows denser while deep loneliness grows more common — the cause lies in the quality of connection, not its quantity. Chronic Low-Grade Depression (dysthymia) is far more prevalent than acute depression; it stays below the threshold of clinical intervention yet continuously lowers quality of life, and in the Polymorphic Society it surfaces fully once no longer masked by survival pressure.
