title_en: First Divergence Phase (ca. 2031–2045): Institutional Responses Fork
summary_en: Under the AI displacement shock, the institutional response speeds of China, the United States, and the Euro-Asian democracies fall in that order; the fork comes from speed differences, not a single node.
overview_en: |
  AI displacement penetrates continuously after 2027 with no single trigger moment; the phase's core is how structural differences in response speed across 2031–2045 push the paths onto different tracks. China's technocratic system deploys its framework without bargaining and responds fastest; the United States moves through successive rounds — expanded unemployment relief, capital-tax and workweek legislation, state-level credit experiments — to a federal BSC framework; Europe's multi-country bargaining is slower still. The Exposure Window is longest in the Global South and does not close by itself — a structural condition, not a bargaining-cost problem — and its accumulated pressure forms the core of the Turbulent Period.
