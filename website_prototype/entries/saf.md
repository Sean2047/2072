title_en: Synthetic Aviation Fuel (SAF)
summary_en: Aviation fuel produced synthetically; once fusion electricity lowers production costs it becomes the principal substitute in conventional aviation.
overview_en: |
  SAF is produced by fusion-powered electrolysis of water for hydrogen, then Fischer-Tropsch synthesis of hydrogen with atmospheric CO₂ into liquid hydrocarbons; chemically identical to conventional jet fuel, usable in existing engines, and forming a closed carbon cycle. Airports are 2072's most complete ground nodes for synthetic fuel — aviation is its largest single source of demand, which keeps airport fueling systems intact through the wave of electrification.
