overview: |
  工业经济需要人们认同同一套成功标准——两百年的结果是全球对"成功"定义高度趋同。当市场成功标准的可及性大幅下降而本地价值体系提供的东西没有消失，本地锚点的相对吸引力自然上升。锚点形态各异（宗教传统、手工技艺、本地声望、关系深度），之间不可换算、无法被外部市场定价——这正是构成稳定内因的关键。人口层面同样趋向自愿均衡。
title_en: Internal Cause Three: Pluralization of Value Anchors (Cultural)
summary_en: Once standardized labor is no longer scarce, the market loses its drive to maintain a "unified standard of success," and suppressed local value logics resurface.
overview_en: |
  The industrial economy needed people to subscribe to a single standard of success — two hundred years of which produced global convergence on the definition of "success." When the attainability of market success falls sharply while what local value systems offer has not disappeared, the relative attraction of local anchors naturally rises. The anchors take varied forms (religious tradition, craft skill, local reputation, depth of relationships), mutually incommensurable and unpriceable by outside markets — precisely what makes them an internal cause of stability. At the population level the tendency is likewise toward a voluntary equilibrium.
