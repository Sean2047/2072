title_en: Evolution of Working Hours and the Dual Possibility of Leisure
summary_en: In the AI era the compression of working hours shifts from something fought for to something allocated; whether leisure turns into creation or void divides along the presence or absence of a meaning framework.
overview_en: |
  The job-disappearance route produces polarized allocation of working hours; the legislated-compression route spreads the remaining work more evenly, with the 24-hour standard workweek established across 2058–2065. The divergence in how time is spent cuts along the presence or absence of a meaning framework, not along class lines. Community is among the most reliable sources of such frameworks: the stronger the cohesion, the higher the probability that leisure turns into creation rather than void — the same 24 hours are two fundamentally different modes of existence in an active community and a dormant one.
