title_en: Internal Cause Two: Systematic Decline of the Drive to Move (Economic)
summary_en: Returns and costs change at the same time and both point toward staying; the contraction of reference frames toward the local is a rational adjustment of self-positioning.
overview_en: |
  On the returns side, AI displacement of transferable jobs shrinks the expected returns to migration to near zero or below. On the cost side, provisioning anchors basic subsistence in any location, turning departure from a necessary escape into a pure option, while local social capital is entirely written off in a move. The double squeeze contracts reference frames naturally; local reputation and network position again become goals both worth pursuing and attainable. The mechanism depends on no one's active push and no information isolation — only on cost-benefit calculation.
