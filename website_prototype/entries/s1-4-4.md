title_en: Mechanism of Worth: Being Needed, Not Priced by the Market
summary_en: The source of a position of being needed shifts from market-priced labor contribution to interpersonal networks within the local community.
overview_en: |
  The local community supplies a system of value confirmation that needs no external certification: everyday mutual aid accumulates into reputation, trust, and standing to receive help — the anchor of polymorphic stability at the level of individual psychology. Its fragility lies in the value network's density depending on population stability; below a critical point the mutual-aid network goes dormant for lack of nodes — the most crucial structural difference between the Forgotten Zones and the Slow Communities. The market economy spent two hundred years releasing individuals from the community; the AI era pushes them back within a single generation.
