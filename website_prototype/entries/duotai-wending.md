title_en: Polymorphic Stability
summary_en: Different institutions, communities, and regions enter different steady states under the same technological shock — a stability of coexistence, not the final victory of any single institution.
overview_en: |
  Polymorphic Stability is not the victory condition of convergence on a single modernity; it is what results when differences in State Integration Capacity (V4) and Historical Lag (V8) send societies into distinct steady states that then coexist. Its anchor in individual psychology is the sense-of-worth mechanism (local communities providing a value-confirmation system that needs no external accreditation); at the cultural level it feeds back through the pluralization of value anchors: the firmer a community's internal value system, the better it retains population, and the more stable the population, the denser the value network.

**词典定义（v156 关键字词典，原文）**
