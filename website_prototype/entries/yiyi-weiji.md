title_en: Meaning Crisis
summary_en: When labor no longer supplies stable identity and value frameworks, individuals face a lasting absence: why am I alive, why am I needed.
overview_en: |
  The Meaning Crisis is first a psychological phenomenon and only then a social one: completion of the L3 institutional response removes the material source of survival anxiety and immediately exposes the collective cultural task of whether my existence is needed, whose completion depends on inherent human psychological adaptability rather than institutional design. The Exposure Window is the crisis's window of highest social intensity; after BSC coverage the intensity drops while the affected population widens — from a secondary problem stacked on survival pressure to a chronic background condition faced in its own right.
